# Zomato-online-order-Analysis-project Summary
This project focuses on analyzing Zomatoâ€™s online food ordering data to understand customer behavior, restaurant performance, and key factors influencing online orders. The analysis aims to uncover actionable insights that can help improve customer experience and business decision-making.

* Objectives

Analyze customer preferences for online ordering vs dine-in

Identify top-performing restaurants and cuisines

Understand the impact of ratings, cost, and location on online orders

Discover ordering trends across different areas and price ranges

* Dataset Overview  

Restaurant name and location

Online order availability

Ratings and number of votes

Average cost for two people

Restaurant type and cuisines


* Key Analysis Performed

Comparison of online order vs offline dining trends

Relationship between ratings and order volume

Analysis of cost for two vs customer preference

Identification of popular cuisines and restaurant types

Area-wise distribution of high-demand restaurants

# Key Insights

Restaurants offering online ordering receive higher customer engagement

Mid-range priced restaurants attract the highest number of orders

Higher ratings strongly correlate with increased online demand

Certain cuisines (e.g., North Indian, Chinese, Fast Food) dominate online orders

Urban locations show significantly higher ordering activity


Conclusion

The analysis highlights that pricing, ratings, and online availability play a crucial role in customer ordering behavior on Zomato. 
These insights can help restaurants optimize pricing strategies, improve service quality, and focus on high-demand cuisines to boost online sales.
